Projects

High-Altitude Balloon Payload

Senior capstone project focused on developing a reaction wheel–stabilized payload to capture in-flight camera footage from near-space altitudes. Contributed to controls development and payload housing design using SolidWorks and MATLAB. Currently transitioning from preliminary design to early prototyping, with plans for a launch and data analysis following the flight test.

Pedal Power

Developed a pedal-powered charging system designed to provide a portable energy source for college students. Led the design and modeling process to convert mechanical energy from cycling into electrical power capable of charging small devices. Built and tested a functioning prototype to validate energy output and durability. The proof-of-concept demonstrated a low-cost, sustainable alternative to commercial systems that are often priced beyond student accessibility.

Custom Made Lightsaber

Designed and fabricated a custom lightsaber as a personal engineering project combining mechanical design and electronic integration. Created concept sketches and CAD models in SolidWorks to refine ergonomics and assembly precision. Soldered and wired internal components, including lighting and sound modules, to achieve synchronized visual and audio effects. The project blended creative design with hands-on prototyping, resulting in a fully functional and visually accurate build.

Modified Theremin

Developed an Arduino-based theremin prototype with a distance sensor to modulate pitch in real time using Pure Data and AM/FM synthesis techniques.

Low-Cost Motion-Activated Faucet Adapter

Engineered an Arduino-powered module to retrofit faucets with motion activation and timed shutoff, integrating 3D printed components designed in SolidWorks.
